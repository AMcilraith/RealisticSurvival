# Gameplay Guide

This page summarizes the major crafting changes in **RealisticCraft**. Most individual recipes are visible in-game; this covers the design rules behind them.

## Materials

- Salvaged Titanium now gives **6 Titanium**.
- Wiring (Copper, Silver, Gold) requires actual metal plus Fiber, Rubber, or Fiber Mesh as insulation/braiding.
- Fiber requires Acidic Raion Pouch; Rubber requires Mild Acid.
- Mild Acid requires Pent instead of Acidic Raion.
- Strong Acid additionally requires Lead.
- Basic Battery additionally requires Pent; Power Cell needs Lead.
- Chips require Coral Shavings.
- Enameled Glass requires Celestine instead of Glass.

## Personal tools

- All tools require **Titanium** (casing) and **Rubber** (grip).
- Electrical tools require **Copper Wire**.
- Tools with moving parts require **Grease**.
- Buoyant tools use the **Water Slug** air bladder.

## Builder / machines

- Electrical machines require a **Chip** (except Fabricator) and **Wiring**.
- Crafting machines require **Sulfur**, **Coral Shavings**, and **Quartz** for laser operation.
- Machines require **Titanium** casing (amount scales with size); glass models also need **Glass**.
- Machines with moving parts require **Grease**.

## Modification Station

- Modification Station and clever modules require **Dedicated Core** and **Wiring Kit**.
- Tool modules also require **Dedicated Core** and **Wiring Kit**.

## Food

- Food recipes and stats reworked to encourage proper meals over cooked fish only.
- Prepared meals provide both **Food** and **Water** in varied proportions.

## Base building

- Light fixtures require **Quartz** instead of Copper.
- Chairs require **Rubber**; tables require **Glass**; signs use **Quartz**; posters require **Lead**.
- Other base recipes have minor ingredient tweaks.
- **Structural base recipes remain largely vanilla** — build large bases without excessive resource cost.
